1880s

Pinar del Río · Santander · Santiago

A family story, told in tobacco leaf.

In the 19th century, Juan Cuevas, a Spanish immigrant from Santander, began what was to be a family business now spanning four generations. Like others fortunate enough to live and work in the Pinar del Río province of Cuba, he commenced cultivating tobacco and turned it into a successful trade.

Following his father's footsteps, Juan Jr. continued and expanded the business, until events in 1959 forced a dramatic change. Years later, in the Cibao Valley of the Dominican Republic, Luis Cuevas Sr. carried on the tradition, handcrafting fine cigars at the family's factory, Tabacalera Las Lavas. Today Luis Sr. is joined by his son, Luis Jr., in the manufacture of premium long-filler cigars in Santiago, Dominican Republic.

After decades of producing cigars for notable brands, the family decided the time had come to release a high-end, premium cigar that would bear the Cuevas name.

Luis Sr. & Luis Jr., walking the rows of their Dominican tobacco fields.

Inside Tabacalera Las Lavas

Everything that happens before the cellophane goes on.

Three commitments that define our Dominican factory, and the family present on the floor every day to enforce them.

01

Tobacco from ten countries.

Our blends draw on leaves from the Dominican Republic, Nicaragua, Peru, Colombia, Ecuador, Brazil, Mexico, Honduras, the United States, and Indonesia, among others. The range is how we hit balance.

02

Every cigar is draw-tested.

Before a single stick leaves the factory it is measured for draw resistance. The result is a smoother, more consistent smoke. No guesswork, no plugged cigars.

03

Frozen before shipping: no beetles. Ever.

Every order is deep-frozen prior to shipping, eliminating the risk of tobacco beetles at any stage. One of the simplest, most effective assurances we can make.

Long-filler cigars use whole, uncut tobacco leaves running the full length of the cigar, as opposed to short-filler (chopped scraps) or medium-filler. Long-filler burns more evenly, draws more consistently, and delivers a cleaner flavor. Every Casa Cuevas cigar is long-filler, hand-rolled at Tabacalera Las Lavas.

Keep cigars in a humidor held at 68–72°F and 65–70% relative humidity. Casa Cuevas cigars are enjoyable right out of the box, but aficionados often rest maduros and full-bodied blends (Reserva Maduro, La Mandarria) for 3–6 months to let the flavors integrate further.
